2015-2016 – baby jess just made an instagram account. i was exploring how to express myself online because i didn't really use social media (at least not-not anonymously) much until then.

baby's first instagram account

for a long time i felt very attached to my instagram feed — @jsctn, my pride and joy. always hovering around 100 posts, with some dating all the way back to 2015, baby jess's slightly timid and moody photos often with the clarendon filter thrown on. my past was something to be proud of, to scroll through mindlessly once a week, and to reminisce of times past.

but i've realized that my affinity for nostalgia might be holding me back. scrolling backwards, i sweetly reminisce of 2019 when i trekked through asia with my good friends, of 2018 when my calendar was packed with creative photoshoots week after week, and 2017 when i was beginning to thrive in the newfound independence of college apartment living. but the scrolling always became bittersweet when i wondered what to post next and how it would compare to the past or fit in with some greater narrative of my life as seen on my profile. in 2020 i began to make websites on the small web, and it was a moment of realization that i could reclaim control on my online persona and narrative through my handmade websites, outside the rules and interfaces of social media platforms. i decided it was time for me to seek closure with my nostalgia.

i still want these snapshots of my happiest and proudest memories to be online, but i realized it couldn't be on my instagram profile. i hope this corner of my website can be a forever-home for these memories, a shrine-like space and a sort of digital photo album where i can honor and move on from the past me.
